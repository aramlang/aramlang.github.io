THE BOOK OF PROVERBS

Chapter 1 1-10 11-20 21-33
[ Proverbs ]


mâthlaw dâshléimon bâr daweedh mâlka deesrayél
The proverbs of Solomon son of David, king of Israel		 1

lmid-dâ' h:ikhemtha wmârdutha wâlmithbây-yanu bmil-lé dsukala
To know wisdom and instruction; and to perceive the words of understanding		 2

wlâmq:âb-balu mârdutha wdhikheltha wzâdeeq:utha wdheena wâthrees:utha
And to receive discipline, and prudent of life, and righteousness,
and justice, and uprightness		 3

lmit-tâl lshâwré 'reemutha wlât:layé eedhâ'tha wthâr'eetha
To give subtlety to the simple, to the young knowledge and discretion		 4

nishmâ' h:âk-keema wnâwsip 'âl h:ikhemthéh wsâkulthana
mdhâbranutha niq:né
A wise man will listen and will add to his wisdom; and a man of
understanding shall attain to leadership		 5

dnishtawdâ' mâthlé wpil-latha wmil-lé d-h:âk-keemé wukhdathhon
To understand proverbs and figures of speech; and the words of
the wise and their riddles		 6

résh h:ekhimtha dikhelthéh dmarya eedhâ'tha déin wmârdutha
shayt:een 'awalé
The reverence of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom, but the ungodly
despise knowledge and instruction 		7

shmâ' bér namosa dawukh wla tit:'é namosa dim-makh
Hear, my son, the law of your father, and forsake not the law
of your mother		 8

yayutha in-non géir lréshakh whâmneekha ls:wrakh
For they are a beauty for your head and a necklace
for your neck  		9

bér in nshâdlunakh khât:-t:ayé la tit-t:pees
My son, if sinners will entice you, consent not		 10
