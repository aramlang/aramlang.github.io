The Construct State
Part I


Malew-thoo-tha: When two words stand in a relation which is expressed by the genitive.

The word,is from the root verb Lawith,
which means, "combined into a unit, or added to".
The root verb in Aramaic is always quoted by the 3rd singular, masculine, past tense; and not by its infinitive.

Adjunct
A word or phrase added to another with the purpose of qualifying or defining. Usually, the word that precedes the particle Dalath.

Governed
A noun that depends upon another, or is controlled by another, usually the word that is preceded by the particle Dalath.

 Confirmative
One noun depends on another directly with the mediation of a preposition such as Dalath, which is attached directly,
"with no space", to the Governed noun.

But when the Adjunct noun is abbreviated, the 
particle Dalath is omitted from the Governed noun.
Sometimes this type of Gdhama is also known as which means Gdhama of a union
or a relationship.

David's house.
"A house that belongs to David."
Bayta D-dhawedh
The word "Bay-ta = House" and "David" are shown in their Full Form, therefore a particle such as "Dalath " must be present between them in order to show a possessive relationship.
But when the first noun "Bay-ta " is abbreviated, Dalath
 must be omitted from the sentence, as shown
in the following examples.
David's house. (Gdhama)
Beith Dawedh
A wooden door.
"A door that is made from wood."
A wooden door. (Gdhama)
The offering of the evening.
"The offering during the evening time."
The offering of the evening. (Gdhama)

The crown of thorns. (Gdhama)
