Formation of the Plural

 Part 8

1- Plural of Paucity & Multitude
In Lesson 67 & 68, you learned about nouns that have
more than one form of plural. As a general rule, each
form is used to denote a certain number of units
in a collection.
« Plural of Paucity - From 1-10 units »
« Plural of Multitude - More than 10 units »
1- Plural of Paucity
[ From 1 - 10 ]
Days Horns Hearts
Example: Four Days
2- Plural of Multitude
[ More than 10 ]
Days Horns Hearts
Example: Forty Days

3- Foreign Nouns
or
A number of foreign nouns from other languages,
especially Greek, are found in Aramaic. 

Nouns ending in, form their plural by removing
the final Simkath from the end of the noun.
(Plur.) -Synod
(Plur.) - Clergyman
Nouns ending in , don't undergo any changes, but
form their plural by placing the plural sign Siamé
above the plural form.
(Plur.) - Heresy
Nouns ending in, form their plural by removing
Alap and replacing it with Simkath.
(Plur.) - Essence
(Plur.) - Church
Nouns ending in .
(Plur.) - Gospel
(Plur.) - Chapter
Some of the foreign nouns having the termination of
Aramaic nouns, form their plural the same way as
the Aramaic nouns.
(Plur.) - Substance, Person
(Plur.) - Bishop
