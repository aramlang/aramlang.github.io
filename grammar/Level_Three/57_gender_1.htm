Gender - (Part - 1)

There are two genders,
masculine and feminine
It is difficult to give rules for determining the gender of a particular noun, but generally:

Nouns ending in are feminine; the converse however is by no means the case.

Church shoe lioness String,
Single hair

Exception: if Tau pertains to the root, the nouns are generally considered masculine.
The following nouns are masculine because their Tau pertains to the root.

Goad,
Spur		 Decoration,
Ornament		 Attention,
Conversation		 Death

Exception: The following nouns are feminine.

Rust, Tartar Foam, Spume

The names of letters of the alphabet, cities and places are considered feminine.
Also, these particular nouns are not ending in, but they are considered feminine .
