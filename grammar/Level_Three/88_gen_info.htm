General Information


Nuq-zé: Dots, pl. masc. Rawer-wé: Large, pl. masc.

Large dots: Are dots used originally in books, which were written without vowels, to distinguish words that have the same spelling, but have different meanings. These dots are used in verbs, nouns, and pronouns as well. Later, these dots were ignored in those books that implemented the vowels system, but were kept in others in addition to the vowels.

There are two kinds of syllables:
Open syllable: When the syllable ends with the sound of a vowel.
Closed syllable: When the syllable ends in a simple consonant.

1- The following examples have Closed syllables. Notice when the letter that follows or (, , ) has no vowel, the syllable ends in a consonant. In this case the vowel is short.
