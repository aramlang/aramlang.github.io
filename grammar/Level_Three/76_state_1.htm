States of Nouns
Gdhama Dash-ma-hé

Gdhama :Means cutting off, contraction of a noun, or the omission of letters in a word.
The word Gdhama is derived from the root verb Gdhum, which means "abbreviated, or cut off ".

Implementation of Gdhama is clearly noticeable in many literatures and poems because it indicates slightly varying significance as well as effect, brevity and
elegance of style and facilitate versification.

According to the Eastern grammarians, a noun may be 
found in three different forms:*

» The Primary Form: ,or
    The Full Form:
This is the full form of the noun with the emphatic Alap at the end of the noun, which is considered to be the sign of determination. This form is called the Emphatic; or the Definite State. This form originally took the place of the definite article "the".

» The First Contracted Form:
This form is called the Absolute State or the Indefinite State.

Bone Astonishment
   
» The Second Contracted Form:
This form denotes a relation between two nouns or dependency of one noun upon the noun that follows. It is also known as the Construct State .
It is divided into two parts:

1 - Abstract
2 - Phonetic
* The European grammarians do not agree with the Eastern grammarians regarding the Primary Form of the noun. They treat the Absolute Form as the Primary Form from which the other two are derived by the addition of proper suffixes.
