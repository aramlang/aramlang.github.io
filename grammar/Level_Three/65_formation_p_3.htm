* As you have noticed from the above examples, there is a small Alap, placed above Yudh, at the beginning of each singular noun. This is a rule whenever a word starts with a vowelless Yudh, a Khwasa is given to Yudh. Since Khwasa is a vowel and not considered as a letter, therefore this vowel has to be preceded by a letter for support; and if there isn't one, Alap "Hamza" is chosen for this purpose.
It should be noted that in the Aramaic language, as in Arabic too, it is a rule not to start a word with a vowel. Unfortunately, books published recently omit the small Alap and that is contrary to the general rule.
