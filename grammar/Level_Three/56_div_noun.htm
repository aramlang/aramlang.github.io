Adjective
Part of a speech used to qualify, define or limit a
substantive; both, the adjective and the substantive, must agree in number and gender.

Famous Bitter Wealthy Sad Long


Described
A substantive is called Mish-tam-hana when it is being followed by an adjective that defines it; both must agree in number and gender.

Leaf Wood Angel Night


Proper Noun
A noun designating some single person, place etc.

Simon Nineveh Babylon Abraham


Common Noun
A noun designating a thing or being which is one of a class.

Eye Soil Ear Eve


Abstract
A noun denoting something intangible, such as the name of a quality.

Paganism Faith Will, Desire Suffering


Collective Noun
A noun designating a number of individuals considered as a group. 

A set of,
Battalion		 People Committee Camp,
Neighborhood
