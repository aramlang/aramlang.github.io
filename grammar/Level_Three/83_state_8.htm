2- Singular nouns ending in

As you have seen previously, all singular nouns ending in
(not in) have the same identical form of Gdhama for both the Construct and the Absolute State.

But when these nouns are ending in, the Construct
Form , is different from the Absolute
Form , as illustrated in the following examples.

1- Described nouns made of more than one syllable, in which either Rwasa or Khwasa vowel precedes Tau, are abbreviated as follow:

Kingdom
  Construct State Absolute State
Full Form
Gdhama
  The Kingdom of God A (one) Kingdom

Belief, Religion, Confession, A thank-offering
  Construct State Absolute State
Full Form
Gdhama

Grace, Goodness, Thank
  Construct State Absolute State
Full Form
Gdhama
  The Grace of our Lord Thank God, I am healthy
