Shlama Am'khon "Peace be with you":

My name is Alan Shlimon Aldawood. I was born in Karkha D'Weith Slokh , or Karkuk as it is known in modern times. Kirkuk is considered one of the ancient cities of Mesopotamia, "Beith Nahreen", Assyria". I was raised in the new suburban area of Kirkuk called Arafa where a large Assyrian community resided and flourished.

In the late 1970s I migrated to The United States of America and settled in Chicago, the "windy city", where, in 1984, I earned a bachelor of science degree in mechanical engineering from the University of Illinois at Chicago. In 1992 I moved to the state of California where I now reside in the small and beautiful city of Modesto. In recent years I have become very interested in the field of computers and the Internet and consequently worked as a lecturer at Modesto Junior College teaching classes in computer graphics, multimedia and web design. 
Currently I devote most of my time to studying theology and researching the writings of the Old Fathers of the Church of the East. I also continue to study my favorite subjects, mathematics and computers.
I have always been inspired and motivated by the countless accomplishments of my great Assyrian ancestors, especially the most revered Old Fathers of the 
Church of the East. Their contributions to the humanities in particular, and to the whole world in general, are profoundly recognized. It would be very difficult indeed for anyone to eradicate their holy role in carrying out the 
message of our Lord to the eastern world.
God Bless.
