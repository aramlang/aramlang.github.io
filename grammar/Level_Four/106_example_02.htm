Lesson 106

Examples - 2 -

mân-nu khza eemama?
Who saw Eemama?
Eemama means "Day" opposite night, it is used
as a proper name (fem.).
 
lmana npâl yâ'q:ow
Why did Jacob fall?
The proper name Jacob is derived from the root verb
(Past tense) 'q:âw, which means,
Held the heel, Searched, Investigated.
Refer to Gen 25:26
 
lâyka iz-zâl sawakh?
Where did your grandfather go?
 
mana khzaw tâm-man?
What did they see there?
 
lâyna béith kreehé?
To which hospital?
 
bâyna shukhlapa?
In which season?
 
bâyda Sha'tha?
In which hour? (fem.)
 
breekh s:âprakh akh daweedh
Good morning my brother David
The word "brother" refers to a close friend or neighbor.
 
kma âkh-khé eeth lakh?
How many brothers do you have?
 
kma âkhwatha eeth lakh?
How many sisters do you have?
 

mon eethaw neesha dshawtaputha deelkhon?
What is the objective of your organization 'company'?
 

mon in-non gawné datha deelkhén?
What are the colors of your flag?
 
mon in-nén....?
What are? (feminine)
 
min âyka?
From where?
 

im-mâth nakhéth mit:ra bâthrakh?
When does it rain in your country?
 

bâthra deel mit:ra nakhéth
In my country the rain falls
 
  bthishryatha bq:âyt:a bârwee'a bsâthwa 
  Fall Summer Spring Winter 
Letter Béith, attached to the front, is a preposition.
It means "During, In"
