Lesson 118

yât-teerutha
Comparative & Superlative Adjectives

I - Comparative Adjectives are expressed by:
1- Including certain adverbs such as:

with preposition(than)

khikhimtha t:awây min 'uthra
Wisdom is better than wealth

Mâtây yâteer khâk-keem min luq:a
Matthew is wiser than Luke

Luq:a bs:eer
  khâk-keem min mâtây
Luke is less wise than Matthew

Luq:a bs:eer
  bkhikhimtha min mâtây
Luke is less in wisdom than Matthew
2- In some situations adverbs are omitted

khiwya 'reemwa min kol-lah khayutha
The serpent was more subtle than all the wild beasts

'âsheen ât min daweedh
You are stronger than David

yâhlân khâelthaneen minkhon
Our battalion is more powerful than yours

mâtây ram min luq:a
Matthew is taller than Luke

mâtây areekh bq:awimtha min luq:a
Matthew is taller in stature than Luke

luq:a z'or bq:awimtha min mâtây
Luke is smaller in stature than Matthew

luq:a kré bq:awimtha min mâtây
Luke is shorter in stature than Matthew
II - Superlative Adjectives are expressed by:
1- Adjective followed by a plural noun
preceded by Béith.

daweedh râb-ba b'âwdé
David is the greatest among servants
2- Adjective followed by a plural noun
preceded by Dalâth.

daweedh râb-ba d'âwdé
David is the greatest of the servants

z'ora dthâlmeedhé
The youngest of the students

z'orhon dthâlmeedhé
The youngest of the students
3- Adjectives followed by
preceded by either Béith or
shâp-peer bkhol
The most beautiful
râb min kol
The greatest of all
