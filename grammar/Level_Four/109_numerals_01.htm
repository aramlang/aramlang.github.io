Lesson 109

Numerals

Part One


Numerals( sing.) in the Aramaic language are nouns that have two forms, a masculine form and a feminine form. 

As in all Semitic languages, the numeral noun stand in
apposition to the noun to which they are attached. In other word, numerals from 3 to 10, the masculine form of the numeral is used with feminine noun, and vise versa. All the masculine forms from 3 to 10 are ending in Alap. Therefore, based on this rule, it is assumed that the final Alap of the masculine numerals was originally Hé replaced later by Alap.

Also, refer to lesson 23 for more information about numerals.

Numerals

Cardinals

Ordinals
shumahé minyanayé

Cardinal number: A number, such as 5, 23, etc. used to indicate quantity but not order.

Ordinal number: A number indicating a position in a series or order. Example: 1st, 2nd, etc.

Ordinals are also known as
(shmahé mithbâytyané), names indicating a relationship between numbers in a series.
Or, (minyané sidhrayé), numbers indicating a position in a series.

Cardinal Numbers
