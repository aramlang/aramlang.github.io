shurara
Confirmation

Confirmation is used for the sake of emphasis, and is expressed by the use of the pronouns, both separable and inseparable, with nouns and verbs.
1 - Using the Personal Pronoun of the 3rd person:
by placing it before the substantive, it gives
greater prominence to it
hoo mâtây
Matthew himself
he ât-tha
The woman (wife) herself
hin-non awahâyk
Your parents themselves
hin-nén akhwathakh
Your sisters themselves
(confirm.)
bréh dalaha
The Son of God 
bra dalaha
The Son of God
(confirm.)
bnaw dluq:a
Sons of Luke
bnây-ya dluq:a
Sons of Luke
(confirm.)
khwârthah dmâryâm
Miriam's girlfriend
khwârtha dmâryâm
Miriam's girlfriend
With prepositions and Dalâth preceding the word being
given greater prominence.
(confirm.)
s:édhâyn dnashuthân
Toward our humanity
s:édh nashuthân
Toward our humanity

lâyt s:édh âkhwathakh mid-dim
There is nothing against your sisters
(confirm.)
lâyt s:édhâyhén dâkhwathakh mid-dim
There is nothing against your sisters
2 - By repeating the 3rd personal pronoun
with the word "kâdh".

hoo kâdh hoo ith-tha lwathân
He himself came to us

'âm-méh kâdh 'âm-méh iz-zâl lshuq:a
With the same person he went to the market

bah kâdh bah bhaie duktha
At that the same place
3 - Using the Personal Pronoun of the
3rd person with verbs

khzeeth lmâlpana
I saw the teacher
(confirm.)
khzeethéh lmâlpana
I saw the teacher
