Detached forms


Part Two


The personal pronoun can be used as a copula verb "to be" in the present tense, provided that it agrees with the subject in gender, number and 'person'.

Some of the personal pronouns are used as enclitics.
Enclitic word pronounced with so little stress that it becomes part of the preceding word. "Webster Dictionary"

The following examples illustrate the changes that occur to the personal pronouns when used as enclitics.
