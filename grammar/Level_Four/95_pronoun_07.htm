Part Six



Relative Pronoun

Relative Pronoun is a word that introduces a subordinate
clause and refers to an antecedent.

The word means conjunctive, from the root verb .

It is represented by the letter Dalath, which is attached
directly to the word that follows it. It is vocalized in the same
way as the inseparable prepositions.

In the following examples, the particle Dalath is used to introduce
a relative clause.

The interrogative and demonstrative pronouns are often
followed by Dalath to give the meaning 'he who', 'she who',
'Those who', etc.

Aram who is from Nineveh.

Sarah who is from Babylon.

He who loves me.

He who doesn't love me.

One whose house I bought.

He whom God has raised did not see corruption.

Those who are in the vineyard.

Those who are without law.

In the word 'dadh-la', the first Dalath represents the relative pronoun, the second Dalath is attached to the word 'La' to give the meaning 'without'.

The trees which are inside the garden.
