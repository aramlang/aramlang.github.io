General Information


In the Assyrian Aramaic language, the genitive relation may be expressed in three ways:

  1- By the use of the emphatic state together with
the preposition Dalath.
 
The crown of the king (kleela dmâlka)
Kleela = Crown, "Emphatic State"
Dalath in front of [mâlka] is a preposition

   2- By the use of the construct state.
 
The crown of the king (kleel mâlka)
Kleel = Crown, "Construct State"

  3- By the use of both the possessive pronominal suffix
and the preposition Dalath.
 
The crown of the king (kleeléh dmâlka)
Kleeléh = His Crown, "Hé is a pronominal suffix"

This example is the most common case, usually when the relation is pure genitive.
   

 


Letter Kap is always aspirated when it is final. Since this is a general rule, therefore, placing a dot below Kap as an indication of being aspirated is optional.

There is one exception to this rule:
Kap is always hard, not aspirated, under both these two conditions:

1-  It is being preceded by Yudh
2-  Yudh is being preceded by Pthakha 


Your books (masc. sing.) (kthawâyk)
As you can see, final Kap is being preceded by Yudh
and Yudh is being preceded by Pthakha.

This rule also applies when Kap is not final, for example:
Upon you (masc. pl.) ('lâykon)
Near you, Against you (masc. pl.) (s:édhâykon).
