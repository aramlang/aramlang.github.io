Lesson 128



Reading Exercise -4-


mâthlé dâkh-kheeq:âr khâk-keema
The proverbs of Akhiq:ar the wise

âkh-kheeq:âr, khâk-keema athoraya, eethaw-wa
sapra dmâlka sânkhéreew, mâlka dathor
Akhiqar, the Assyrian wise man, was
the scribe of king Sennacherib, king of Assyria

bér! t:aw lâmhâp-pakhu képé 'âm gâwra khâk-keema
min dâlmishta khâmra 'âm gâwra sâkhla
My son! it is better to remove stones with a wise man
than to drink wine with a fool.

bér! lâwit:tha dawukh wdhim-makh la tâyté
dâlma bt:awatha dâwnâyk la tikhdé
My son! bring not upon you the curse of your
father and your mother,
lest you will not rejoice in the blessings of your children.

bér! t:aw 'weer b'âynaw min haw dâ'weer blib-béh
(dâ'weerib-lib-béh)
'weer 'âyné dén bâ'ghâl yalép urkha wazél bah
wâ'weer lib-ba shawéq: urkha trees:ta wazél bthâwsha
My son! better is he that is blind of eye than he that is
blind of heart;
for the blind of eye straightway learns the road and
walks in it:
but the blind of heart leaves the right way and
goes astray (into desert).

t:aw rakhma dq:areew min âkh-kha drâkh-kheeq:
wt:aw shma t:awa min shupra sâg-geeîa
mit:ol dâshma t:awa q:aîém l'alâm
wshupra balé wmithkhâb-bâl
Better is a friend who is near than a brother who is far away.
And better is a good name than much beauty:
because a good name stands forever
and beauty wanes and wastes away.

t:awây khdha s:ipra dweedhakh min alép dparkhan
t:awa miskénutha dâmkhânsha min 'uthra dâmwâd-dâr
Better is one sparrow in your hand than a thousand flying
Better is poverty that gathers than wealth that scatters

bér! 'âynéh dwârnasha âkh mâb-bu'a dmây-ya
wla saw'a nikhsé 'dhâm-ma dmâlya 'âpra
My son! the eye of man is like a fountain of water,
and it is not satisfied with riches until filled with dust

bér! bâr 'ât-teeré ikh-khâl khiwya wamreen
lâsyutha âkhléh, wâkhléh bâr miskéna wamreen
dâlkhâpnéh âkhléh
My son! a wealthy man ate a snake, and they say
he ate it for healing (medicine), and the poor man ate it and
they say for his hunger he ate it.
