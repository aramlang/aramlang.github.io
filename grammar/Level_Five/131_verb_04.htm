Lesson 131



Examples


lawa hana ri'yana dâwraham
This was not the opinion of Abraham

wnihpokh lâthréh âkh dâhwa
And he shall return to his place as he was

hwaw léh bnây-ya tlatha
Three sons were born to him

khâmish khâkeemanwây
Five were wise (fem.)

nihwé géir haydéin uls:ana râb-ba
âyna dla hwa min résheethéh d'alma
wâ'dhâm-ma lhasha wla nihwé

For then there will be great suffering
such as has never happened from the beginning of the world
until now, and never will be again. 
1- wma 'âm knathéh hawé
And when he is with his companion
2- wma 'âm knathéh hawéwa
And when he was with his companion
1- wma 'âm knathah hawya
And when she is with her companion
2- wma 'âm knathah hawyawa
And when she was with her companion

ara lawa ât eethâyk haw râb khâyla?
Were not you "yourself - are" that commander?

wâhwath kul-la âr'a lish-shana khâdh wmâmla khâdh

And the whole earth was of one language and of one speech.

wâhwa dkhâdh shq:âl min mâdhinkha
ishkâkh pq:â'tha bâr'a dsin'ar weethiw tâm-man

And it came to pass, as they journeyed from the East
they found a plain in the land of Sin'ar and they settled there.

w-wâdâr in-non marya min tâm-man
'âl âp-pây kol-lah âr'a
wpash min dâlmiwna q:reetha
mit:-t:ol hana q:ra shmah bawél
mit:-t:ol dthâm-man bâlbil marya lish-shana dkhol-lah âr'a

And the Lord scattered them abroad from there
upon the face of all the earth
and they left off to build the city
for this, he called her name Babel
for there the Lord confounded the language of all the earth.
In certain cases, the perfect of "hwa" is used with the
Active Participle "present" to convey the sense of imperative;
usually used for emphasis.

hwâyton msâibreen uls:anâykon

Be patient in "your" tribulations (masc. pl.)
Endure, bear your tribulations

---------------------

Or, with the present of "hwa"

walé lakh dthihwé khpeet:a wla khwin-nana
You must be diligent and not slothful. (masc. sing.)
Verb "hwa" doesn't require to be repeated, when it refers
to several participles.

hin-néin déin bakhyanwây kâdh la yadh'an ...mana hwa

For they were crying while not knowing
what happened. (fem. pl.)
