Lesson 127


Reading Exercise -3-

*
ra'ya wghân-nawa
The shepherd and the thief (gân-nawa)

ra'ya khâdh eethwa dhakhân-na miskénawa
There was a shepherd who was poor

âykân-na dlâytwa léh mid-dim
dnizbin zawga khâdh dâmsané
so that he didn't have anything, so to buy a pair of shoes.

bkhâdh dén min yawmeen kâdh mhâl-likhwa
Then during one day when he was walking

khipyaya bthâlga wza-îâ'-wa min q:ursha
bare foot in the snow and he was shaking from sever cold

ithq:arâw lwathéh gân-nawa nash wim-mâr léh
A thief approached "became near" him and said to him

o ra'ya khzee kma sneeq: ât
wlâyt lakh ap msané
O shepherd, look how needy you are and don't have even shoes!

'widh âkhwath, gnu whawé lakh
sâg-gee kispa
act like me, steal, and you have a lot of money

wmishkâkh ât dthizbin kol ma ds:awé ât
and you be able to buy whatever you desire

khas lee, pân-nee ra'ya, rkhâq: mén
God forbid! replied the shepherd, go away from me

myâtra lee miskénutha min 'uthra dghân-nawutha
It is better for me poverty than the
wealth "gained" from stealing
