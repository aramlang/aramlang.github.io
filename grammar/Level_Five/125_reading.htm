Lesson 125


Reading Exercise -1-

*
keesa âb-beedha
The missing bag "pouch"

gâwra 'ât-teera kâdh azélwa bkhâdh yom lshuq:a
A wealthy man, while he was going,
during one day, to the market.

npâl min-néh keesa mlé zuzé wla eedhâ' béh
A pouch fell from him, full of money,
and he didn't know about it.

wâkhza shâwra z'ora lkheesa* wshâq:léh

* lkeesa, Kap, in vernacular is never aspirated
A small boy saw the pouch and he took it.

wârhit: bathâr 'ât-teera kâdh q:a'é
And he ran after the wealthy man while shouting.

dadha dadha* ha keesa dânpâl min-nakh
Uncle, uncle*, behold the pouch that fell from you.

* Used as a title of respect.

wâshq:âl 'ât-teera lkheesa wâwdee lshâwra
And the wealthy man took the pouch and thanked the boy.

wkhâdh s:wa dnit-til-léh mid-dim, la q:âb-bil shâwra
And he desired to give him something, the boy refused.

wkhar béh 'ât-teera wim-mâr
And the wealthy man looked at him and said.

t:uw-wa lawahé deethlhon bnây-ya t:awé âkh hana!
Blessed are the parents who have good children like this one.
*Acknowledgement
Assyrian Reading, Book 2
Fr. Albert Abuna Archbishop: Aprem Gewargis Alkhuri Sleewo Jibbo Shimun
