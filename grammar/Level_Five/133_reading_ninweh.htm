Lesson 133



Reading Exercise

wâhwa pithghaméh dmarya 'âl yawnan
And the word of the Lord came to Jonah

dthârtéin zâwneen lmémâr
the second time, saying

q:om zil lneenwé mdheeta râbtha
Arise, go to Nineveh, the great city

wâkhriz 'léih karozutha damârna lakh
and proclaim to it the proclamation which I tell you

wq:am yawnan wiz-zâl lneenwé âkh milthéh dmarya
And Jonah arose and went to Nineveh, according to the
word of the Lord

wneenwé mdheethawath râbtha lalaha
And Nineveh was a great city to God

mardé tlatha yawmeen
a three days' journey

wsharee yawnan lmi'-'âl lneenwé mardé yawma khâdh
And Jonah started to enter into Nineveh a day's journey

wâkhriz wim-mâr mik-ka larb'een yawmeen neenwé mithhâpka
And he proclaimed and said: within forty days Nineveh
becomes overthrown

whâymin nashéih dneenwé balaha
And the people of Nineveh believed in God

wâpsâq: s:awma wâlwish sâq:-q:é
And decreed a fast and put on sackcloth

min rawerwanâyhon wâ'dhâm-ma lâz'orâyhon
from the greatest of them and even their least
wâmt:ath miltha lmâlka dneenwé
and the news reached the king of Nineveh

wq:am min kurse-yéh wâshq:âl taghéh min-néh
he arose from his throne and took off his crown from
himself

wâlwish sâq:-q:a weethiw 'âl q:it:ma
and put on sackcloth and sat down in ashes

wâkhriz wim-mâr bneenwé min puq:dana (pugdana)
dmâlka wâdhrawerwanaw
and they proclaimed and said in Nineveh: by the decree of
the king and his nobles
bnâynasha wâw'eera wthawré w'ana la nit:e'mun mid-dim
men and beast and herd and flock will not taste anything

wla nir'on apla mây-ya nishton
and will not feed not even will drink water

il-la nithkâs-son sâq:-q:é bnâynasha wâw'eera
but will be covered with sackcloth men and beast

wniq:ronéh lalaha bâkhnâghtha wnihpokh nash
and they will cry to God with groaning, and every man
will turn
min urkhéh beeshta wmin kht:upya deeth beedhaw
from his evil way and from the violence that is in his hands

mân yadhâ' in mithpné alaha wâmrâkhim 'lâyn
who knows if God will return and have mercy upon us

wmâhpikh min-nân khimtha drughzéh dla néwâdh *
and turn away from us His fierce anger, that we may
perish not

wâkhza alaha 'wadhâyhon dthaw min urkhath-hon beeshatha
and God saw their works that they turned from their evil ways

 wâhpikh minhon khimtha drughzéh wla awbidh in-non
He turned away from them His fierce anger, and He did not destroy them
* Verbs that start with Alâp or Yodh do not follow the
general rule. Please refer to lesson 134
Grammar of the Aramaic language
Fr. Yawsip Q:il-laita, page 21
