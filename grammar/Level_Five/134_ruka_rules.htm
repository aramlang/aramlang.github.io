Lesson 134



Rukakha and Q:ushaya

Please refer to lessons 122, 124

General Rules

1- The perfect of the ( p'âl )

The 1st radical is always hard (Q:ushaya)
The 2nd radical is soft (Rukakha)

2- The imperfect of the ( p'âl )

When preceded by âmnâth 
The 1st radical is soft (Rukakha)
The 2nd radical is hard (Q:ushaya)


Exceptions

[a]The perfect of verb starting with
a strong Alâp ikh-khâl (he ate)

When preceded by âmnâth
The 2nd radical is soft (Rukakha)

[b]The perfect of verb starting with
Yodheewish (he dried up, withered)

When preceded by âmnâth
The 2nd radical is soft (Rukakha)

[c]The perfect of Double é
 bâz (he plundered, despoiled)

When preceded by âmnâth
The 1st radical is hard (Q:ushaya)

[d] The perfect of Double é
râth (he trembled)

When preceded by âmnâth
The 2nd radical is soft (Rukakha)

[e] The perfect verb starting
with a vowel
barikh (he blessed)

When preceded by Alâp
The 1st radical is hard (Q:ushaya)
and Alâp takes Zlama Q:âshya
