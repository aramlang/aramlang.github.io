Lesson 40

Syamé : Two large horizontal dots above a word that denote the plural in nouns, adjectives, and certain forms of the verb.
Originally these points were used in books which were written without vowels to distinguish the plural from the singular. Later, these points were kept even in those books which had the vowels.
By reference they are put over Résh and Dalâth if these letters occur in the word, whether they already have a vowel or not. But they may be put over any letter that does rise above the line. When written over Résh one of them counts as the dot of the letter itself, as .
If Résh occurs twice in a word, Syamé are placed on the second Résh.
e.g. shareeré, Faithfuls (Plur. masc.).


- Rules for Syamé -
They are placed:
over the plural forms of all nouns:
  gâwré yawmatha
Men Days
They are placed over the plural forms of all adjectives in their absolute state: as in the 2nd example, which shows Syamé used with the adjective "bthuleen", which means "Virgins".

But not when they imply also the verb: as in the 1st example, "bthuleen" is without Syamé.
  1.
dâyrayé bthuleen
 2.
ith-thaw dâyrayé bthuleen
Monks are virgins Virgin monks came
over collective nouns: Their forms do not alter whether they are in the plural or the singular form.
 
râkhsha
râkhsha
also, râh:sha
see lesson 8
'ana
Horses Creeping insects Sheep, flock
Over nouns which are used only in the plural forms.
  mây-ya dmây-ya khây-yé
Water Ransom, Price Lives
over certain prepositions with plural forms.

Only books written in the Western Aramaic form follow the above rule.

Syamé are not shown in the following examples:
  bâlkhodhâyhon khdharâyhon
Alone (Plur. masc.) Around them (Plur. masc.)
over numerals from 2 to 10.
  trâyhon târtâyhén
Both (masc.) Both (fem.)
They are placed over the 3rd Plur. fem. of the perfect tense and the 3rd and 2nd Plur. fem. of the imperfect tense of the verb.
