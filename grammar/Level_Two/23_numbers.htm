Lesson 23

The objective of this lesson is to introduce to you the old Aramaic system of numeration adopted in the old manuscripts published in the past. Recently, this system is almost obsolete and has been superseded by the western numeral system.

In some manuscripts an alternate method is used for numbers 100 to 900. By placing a dot above the letter its value multiplies by 10.

Example:= 500 , = 800
An oblique line placed above a letter multiplies its
value by 1000.

Example:= 1000


Some manuscripts use a different notation for this.

Example:= 1000
A small horizontal line placed below a letter multiplies its value by 10,000.

Example:= 10,000
Two oblique lines subjoined to a letter, multiplies its value by 10,000,000.

Example:= 10,000,000
Usually written double when standing alone.
