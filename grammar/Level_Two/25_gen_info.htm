Lesson 25

General Information


Assyrian letters are written and read from right to left. Each letter should be written by beginning at the right hand bottom corner, and most of them in several separate strokes. I have demonstrated this concept previously in lessons (1 - 22).


The Estrangela letters are now as a rule only used for headings of documents, chapters, titles of books, etc. There are no capitals, and the Estrangela letters are not used for this purpose.


The alphabet can be read thus:

âbgâd   hâw-wâz   khât:-t:ee   klmân   s:â'pâs:  q:ârshât


All letters are affixed to the preceding letter; and all also to the
following letter except in certain cases . (Please, refer to lesson 24.)
Estrangela letters follow the same joining rule as in the Assyrian or
Jacobite scripts.


When are final, whether joined to the preceding letter or not, a small strokeis added to the end of the letters, thus they become .....


Final Yodh after Hé is generally written in manuscripts.


A mark [ ~ ], called Majliyana, is used with certain consonants to give each one a different sound. Majliyana is not found in the Assyrian Aramaic language.

When it is under Gamâl, it changes the pronunciation from
g (in game) to g (in gender) or j (in jay), and therefore Gamâl is called Jamâl instead.

When it is under Kap , it changes the pronunciation from k to ch (in church), and therefore Kap is called Chap instead.

The same mark with Zâynor Sheen , makes them pronounced as s in pleasure.
