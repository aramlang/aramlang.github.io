Lesson 44
Examples - 7 -

The eagle seizes a sparrow
nishra khat:ép s:ipra
  3 2 1  
1- eagle: noun.
2- seize: masc. , singular, 3rd person, present tense.
3- sparrow: fem. noun.

Sargon eats pomegranate
sârgon akhél rum-mana
  3 2 1  
1- Sargon: masc. proper name.
2- eat: masc. , singular, 3rd person, present tense.
3- pomegranate: masc. noun.

Susan takes off her shoes
shoshân shalkha msané
  3 2 1  
1- Susan: fem. proper name.
2- take off: fem., singular, 3rd person, present tense.
3- shoes: masc. noun.

The pupil plays in the courtyard
tâlmeedha sha'é bdharta
  3 2 1  
1- pupil: masc. noun.
2- play: masc. , singular, 3rd person, present tense.
3- in courtyard: Béith is a preposition = in

The boy knocks on the door
t:âlya t:arép 'âl târ'a
  4 3 2 1  
1- boy: masc. noun.
2- knock: masc. , singular , 3rd person, present tense.
3- on: preposition, not to be confused with the verb Entered. See Lesson 43, 1st example.
4- door: noun.

The girl carries a cat on her shoulder
t:leetha t:'na q:ât:u 'âl kâthpah
  5 4 3 2 1  
1- girl: fem. noun.
2- carry: fem. , singular, 3rd person, present tense.
3- cat: fem. noun.
4- on: preposition
5- her shoulder:  He' at the end is a pronoun = her.
