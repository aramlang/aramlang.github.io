Lesson 42

Aspirated Letters

The letters(sometimes indicated by the mnemonic word bghâdh-kpath  ), have a twofold pronunciation, a hard sound (original) and a soft sound.

The soft or aspirated sound is called Rukakha, and the hard or unaspirated sound is called Q:ushaya.

The hard form is indicated by placing a small dot over the letter (omitted in modern printed books). The soft form is indicated by placing a dot under the letter. But these dots are not written when they could interfere with vowel points.

There is one exception. The letter Pé is written when aspirated. A dot is never placed over or under the letter Pé (Eastern Aramaic).

Rules for Aspiration: This subject is very broad and can be mastered only through experience. Therefore, I will discuss these rules in more detail later on in lessons 48-52 .
