Examples - 5 -

Please click each sentence to listen to the pronunciation.

The camel ate grass
gâmla ikh-khâl gil-la
  3 2 1  
1- camel: noun (masc. & fem.)
2- ate: past tense, masculine, singular, 3rd person.
3- grass: noun, also Hay.

The sun rose
shimsha dnâkh
  2 1  
1- sun: noun (masc. & fem.)
2- rose: past tense, masculine, sing., 3rd person .

The elephant lives in the woods
peela khayé b'awa
  3 2 1  
1- elephant: noun ( masc.)
2- live: present tense, masc., sing., 3rd person.
3- in wood: Beith is a preposition = in

Joseph wrote with a pencil
yawsip kthâw bq:ânya
  3 2 1  
1- Joseph: proper noun (masc.) 
2- wrote: past tense, masc., sing. , 3rd person. 
3- with pencil: Beith is a preposition = with

The pupil went to school
iz-zâl tâlmeedha lmâdhrâshta
  3 2 1  
1- went: past tense, masc., sing. , 3rd person. 
2- pupil: noun (masc.)
3- to school: Lamadh is a preposition = to

The boy woke up from his sleep
t:âlya q:am min shintha
  4 3 2 1  
1- boy: noun (masc.)
2- woke up: past tense, masc., sing. , 3rd person.  
3- from: written without a vowel except for a dot placed under it (refer to lesson 88).
4- sleep: noun.
