Lesson 47

Rules for pronunciation
of certain words
There are certain situations when the sound of a particular letter is slightly modified. These exceptions require more attention during reading or writing the language.
It has been found that, in some situations, certain syllables are somewhat difficult to pronounce. This is attributed to the fact that particular letters happen to be together in the same syllable. Therefore, the sound of one of the letters is slightly modified to ease and overcome the difficulty in pronunciation.

In this lesson, I have presented a few examples that will assist you in learning these rules.
Students who have acquired some familiarity with the language will have no difficulty in learning these rules.

When a vowelless Zain is followed by either:
Kap or Q:op, both have vowels, Zain is pronounced as Simkâth.
  sq:apa, (not zq:apa)
A stake, the vowel Sq:apa.
  skhârya (not zkhârya)
Zechariah, a person's name.

When a vowelless Simkâth is followed by either:
Béith or Gamâl, both have vowels and unaspirated,
Simkâth is pronounced as Zain.
  'izba (not isba)
Grass, green herb.
  nizgé (not nisgé)
Will increase (verb).

When Simkâth with a vowel is followed by Pé,
Simkâth is pronounced as S:adhé.
  s:ipra (not sipra)
Book, document, record.

When a vowelless Q:op is followed by:
A hard Dalâth that has vowel, Q:op is pronounced as Gamâl.
  pugdana (not puq:dana)
Command, order
  yâgdana (not yâq:dana)
Burning, conflagration, a fire

When a vowelless Q:op is followed by:
Either Tau or Pé, both have vowels, Q:op is pronounced as Kap.
  in-nâktha (not In-nâq:tha)
Grief, sorrow, groan

When aspirated Gamâl is followed by Pé that
has a vowel, Gamâl is pronounced as Khéith.
  bâkhpit-ta (not bâghpit-ta)
Inside the vine.
