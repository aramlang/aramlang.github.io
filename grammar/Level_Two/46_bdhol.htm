mâplatha
The letters are called Mâp-latha because they 'fall' before, or precede a word and modify its signification.
= in, with = of = and = to, for, of

In the following table, I have given two different examples that explicitly show, when one or two or more of Bdhol preced a word, the modifications that is taking place to its significance.
Also, as they precede a word some of them receive Pthakha, and some don't.

Special Case:
As mentioned previously, When Alâp is at the beginning of a word and is preceded by one of the Bdhol letters (prefix), the vowel falls off and gives its vowel sound to the prefix. In other word, Alâp surrenders its vowel to the prefix in pronunciation and not in writing, therefore, the word sounds as if the prefix has the vowel, as shown below.
urkha  Road, a way
Alâp has Rwas:a
burkha In the way
Béith receives the Rwas:a
dwurkha (He/They) who (is/are)
In the way
Dalâth is without Pthakha
Compare the above case with "khâq:la" example.
Letters (prefix) in red are aspirated.
   
»We conclude that when a word starts with a consonant, bdhol will receive Pthakha, and vise versa.

» If more than one prefix Bdhol are preceding a word, every other prefix Bdhol will receive Pthakha, provided that the first rule is being followed first.
