Lesson 45

General Information


Letter Alap has no pronunciation of its own, but it is used to hang a vowel on, or as a quiescent final letter. It has only the sound of the vowel which it receives.
Alap is pronounced like the Arabic Hamza:

Examples:
1- At the beginning of a word.
ith-tha He Came
2- In the Middle of a word.
shîil He asked
3- At the end of a few verbs.
t:mâî Became defiled
When Alap is at the beginning of a word and is preceded by one of the Bdhol letters (prefix), the Hamza falls off and gives its vowel sound to the prefix.
dalaha of God, Pertains to God

Doubling letters 'peeputh athwatha.
In some situations a single consonant is pronounced as if it was written twice, without placing any sign to indicate that.

When a consonant has a vowel and is preceded by Pthakha.
t:âb-bakha [ ] A cook, A butcher
zâd-deeq:a [ ] Righteous 

Exception:
alaha God awa Father
Also, When a consonant has a vowel and is preceded by Zlama Psheeq:a.
im-ma [] Mother, Source
ib-ba [] Fruit


In certain situations, Pthakha sounds like Sq:apa.
If either Résh or`E, comes after Pthakha, then Pthakha is pronounced like Sq:apa.
sha-reera Honest, True
Ba-rikh He Blessed
ra-'ee Reconciled,
Contemplated
Exception:
Shârwayutha Recentness, Commencement
